Transfer Capabilities Assessment

April 2025

Question and Response

Question: Is it feasible now for NVC Banking Platform to send funds, make payment, transfer money from our platform to another bank through SWIFT, ABA fedwire, ACH at this point? We do not currently have our own SWIFT Code however.

Yes, the NVC Banking Platform is technically capable of sending funds and making transfers to other banks through multiple channels, even without having your own SWIFT code. Here's how the platform currently supports various transfer mechanisms:

SWIFT Transfers (as an originator):
  • The platform can generate and format SWIFT messages (MT103, MT202, etc.)
  • These transfers would be processed through partner banks who have SWIFT membership
  • Your platform acts as the originator, but the actual SWIFT transmission happens via a correspondent banking relationship
Server-to-Server Transfers:
  • The system supports direct server-to-server connections with partner financial institutions
  • These transfers use secure API endpoints and digital signatures for authentication
  • This is particularly useful for high-volume institutional transfers
RTGS (Real-Time Gross Settlement):
  • The platform can interface with central bank RTGS systems through partner banks
  • These transfers are settled in real-time during banking hours
  • Several major countries' RTGS systems are supported, including those in Nigeria, South Africa, UAE, etc.
EDI (Electronic Data Interchange):
  • The platform supports standardized electronic fund transfers with financial institutions
  • Multiple EDI formats (X12, EDIFACT) are supported for compatibility

The current architecture uses a "pass-through" model where NVC Banking Platform prepares the transfer information and routes it through partner financial institutions who have the necessary memberships and codes.

To enhance these capabilities, you might consider:

  1. Establishing more formal correspondent banking relationships
  2. Applying for your own SWIFT membership (though this is a lengthy process)
  3. Pursuing direct integrations with ACH and Fedwire networks

Implementation Details

The NVC Banking Platform implements these transfer capabilities through a modular architecture that separates channel-specific implementations while maintaining a consistent API:

  • SWIFT Integration: Handles message formatting according to SWIFT standards (MT103, MT202, etc.) and tracks message state through the correspondent banking workflow.
  • Server-to-Server Module: Provides secure API endpoints and client libraries for direct institutional integration.
  • RTGS Integration: Connects to central bank RTGS systems through approved channels and manages settlement timing.
  • EDI Processing Engine: Converts between internal data formats and EDI standards for electronic transfers.

Each of these systems is accessible through the unified NVC Banking Platform interface, ensuring consistent user experience despite the different underlying mechanisms.
