Agreement Overview

The NVC Fund Bank Asset Management Agreement establishes the formal relationship between NVC Fund Bank (as the asset manager) and your institution (as the client). This agreement outlines the rights, responsibilities, and obligations of both parties in the asset management relationship.

As a Supranational Sovereign Financial Institution with a balance sheet of over $10 trillion in assets, NVC Fund Bank provides comprehensive asset management services to qualified institutional clients worldwide. Our agreement is designed to establish clear guidelines for the management of your assets while ensuring compliance with international standards and regulations.

1. Scope of Services

  • Discretionary investment management authority
  • Portfolio construction and management
  • Asset allocation strategies
  • Risk management procedures
  • Performance measurement and reporting
  • Regular portfolio reviews
  • Investment policy statement implementation

2. Investment Guidelines

  • Investment objectives and strategies
  • Asset allocation parameters
  • Permissible investments
  • Investment restrictions and prohibitions
  • Risk tolerance parameters
  • Benchmark selection and tracking
  • ESG investment considerations

3. Fees and Expenses

  • Performance-based fees (if applicable)
  • Fee calculation methodology
  • Expense allocation provisions
  • Billing frequency and procedures
  • Third-party fee disclosure
  • Fee adjustment mechanisms

4. Reporting and Communication

  • Regular reporting requirements
  • Performance measurement standards
  • Client meetings and review schedule
  • Special reporting requests
  • Communication protocols
  • Online portal access
  • Confidentiality provisions

5. Legal Framework

  • Fiduciary responsibilities
  • Standard of care
  • Liability provisions
  • Indemnification clauses
  • Proxy voting authority
  • Regulatory compliance requirements
  • Dispute resolution mechanisms

6. Term and Termination

  • Agreement duration provisions
  • Termination conditions and notice periods
  • Termination without cause provisions
  • Material breach definitions
  • Asset transition procedures
  • Final accounting requirements
  • Post-termination obligations
